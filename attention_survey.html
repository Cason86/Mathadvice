Attentional Control Scale

1. It is difficult for me to focus my attention on a difficult task when there is noise around me.

2. It is difficult for me to focus my attention when I need to concentrate on a problem.

3. When I am trying to do something, things around me can still distract me.

4. Even when music is playing in the room around me, I can still be attentive.

5. When I am fully focused, I can concentrate so hard that I cannot realise what is happening around me.

6. When I am reading and researching, I am easily distracted if someone is talking in this room.

7. When I am trying to focus my attention on something, I have trouble stopping distracting thoughts.

8. When I am excited, I have a hard time focusing my attention.

9. When I am fully focused, I ignore feelings of hunger and thirst.

10. I can switch from one task to another quickly.

11. It takes me a while to really engage with a new task.

12. When writing notes in class, I have difficulty coordinating my attention between writing and listening.

13. I quickly become interested in a new topic if I need to.

14. Reading or writing while I am on the phone is easy for me.

15. It is difficult for me to carry on two conversations at the same time.

16. It is difficult for me to come up with a new idea quickly.

17. After being interrupted or distracted, I can easily turn my attention to what I was trying to do before.

18. When a distracting idea comes to mind, I can easily take my attention away from it.

19. Taking turns doing two different tasks is easy for me.

20. Breaking out of one way of thinking and looking at it from a different perspective is hard for me.
