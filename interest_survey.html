Mathematics Interest Scale for Students (MIS-St)

1. I’m more used to deciding things by calculating the probability, likelihood, or comparing the pros and cons of each thing.

2. It’s a great feeling for me to find out my mistakes in maths and fix them by solving them.

3. Maths classes are fun.

4. I would like to join a maths or maths-related club or society.

5. I want to be a mathematician when I graduate.

6. I prefer to do the maths myself rather than asking the teacher directly.

7. I like to repeat calculations to make sure I get the right result.

8. I like maths lessons.

9. I find some maths videos or documentaries interesting.

10. It is nice to work in a maths lab.

11. I prefer to do my own maths rather than reading steps and explanations.

12. I am curious about using maths knowledge.

13. Schools should increase the number of maths lessons.

14. I would like to have a maths book or a maths toy (e.g. abacus, jigsaw puzzle, etc.).

15. Teaching maths is a fun job.

16. I don’t readily accept other people’s results until I have worked them out myself.

17. It is important for me to learn new things about maths.

18. Maths lessons never bore me.

19. I often read books about mathematics during holidays.

20. A mathematician’s job is never boring.

21. The best way for me to accept a mathematical theorem is to work out the result myself using a known theorem, rather than looking directly at a textbook.

22. When doing mathematical calculations, I like to explore different ways of solving problems and try out new ways of solving them.

23. The preparation of maths textbooks and the use of related materials (geometry boards, polygonal aids, etc.) are interesting.

24. In addition to completing my homework, I learn some extracurricular mathematical knowledge and formulas at home.

25. Being a mathematician gives people a better view of me.

26. I don’t hesitate to challenge myself when I am faced with a mathematical problem.

27. When there is evidence that my calculations may be wrong, I think about how to verify and improve my calculations.

28. I always look forward to maths lessons.

29. It is fun to talk about maths with my friends after school.

30. I would like to pursue a career in maths research, even if it would take me many more years of education.

31. It is more important for me to understand a mathematical concept thoroughly than to study it just for a test.

32. I am happy to listen to other people’s advice when I am consistently unable to work out the correct result.

33. If I had to choose my own school, whether or not the maths classes were varied and interesting would be an important criterion for me to choose.

34. If I could, I would like to visit a maths laboratory on holiday.

35. Becoming a maths-related worker would be of great value to me.
